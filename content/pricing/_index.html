All plans include all features, unlimited users, and reasonable API and bandwidth usage. WebSocket connections stands for concurrent WebSocket connections.

Why there's no free plan?

StaticBackend is a one-man self-funded company. Paid plans ensure the company continues to innovate and offers the best service and support possible. Unfortunately, a free plan is not possible at the moment.

Need more?

We recommend investigating dedicated managed services. You may also look at the self-hosted services.

How database size calculated?

We're using PostgreSQL and its size on disk feature to calculate database sizes. That includes all indexes you may create and the data itself.

Make sure to perform regular cleanup of your data, especially for users that are not using your product anymore. That's an easy way to keep your database size small.

What are reasonable limits?

We currently don't enforce a hard bandwidth limit. We can decline a good rule of thumb from the database size in the plan. For instance, 4GB database size would give roughly 2GB of bandwidth per month.

The API calls are throttled per user. A user cannot perform more than 25 API calls in 60 seconds.

What if we exceed the database size?

You'll receive an automated email at 90% of your capacity to prepare for upgrading your plan or clean up some documents. If you exceed your limit, we'll contact you to find the right option for you.

Your users are never impacted. Your account can continue to write and read data while you upgrade.

You cannot exceed WebSocket connections

We are having a hard limit on WebSocket connections. You cannot exceed the maximum of concurrent connection your plan allow. You'll receive an HTTP error if you try to connect and your connection pool is full.

We recommend you maintain an internal count inside your application so you may notify yourself if you're often approaching your maximum connection.
